Schedule and Info

All events will take place in Arlington, Virginia
The closest airport is Washington National (DCA). It is easily accessible to Arlington by public transit. Dulles (IAD) is the next best. It is about 45 minutes away by car or bus. Baltimore-Washington International (BWI) works, but it can take 1-2 hours to get to Arlington by car or 2+ hours by public transportation.

Lodging

While there may be cheaper options out there, we have blocks of rooms reserved at two Arlington hotels at a discounted rate: the Courthouse Hilton and Inns of Virginia. We picked these hotels based on perceived convenience for our guests but you should feel free to stay wherever you would like. E-mail us for a more personal recommendation. With advanced notice we may also be able to arrange home stays for those of you traveling from afar on limited budgets. If you live in the area and are willing to house guests, please let us know.

The Courthouse Hilton

The Hilton is located very close to Courthouse Metro on the Orange Line. It is also across the street from a lovely Saturday farmer's market and within walking distance of many bars and restaurants. A free shuttle to and from the wedding site will also be available for guests of the hotel. It is a short cab ride, a twenty-five minute walk, or a two-stop metro ride to Corinne's parents' house, which is the venue for Friday night's reception. The cost per night is $129 plus taxes (around $145 total). To make a reservation in our room block follow this link.

Inns of Virginia, Arlington

The Inns of Virginia is a budget-minded hotel that is just a twenty minute walk from the wedding venue. All the rooms have two queen-sized beds and there is free onsite parking. It is about a ten minute drive from Friday night's reception venue and a 1.5 mile walk from the Metro. We have held ten rooms here for $89.99/night plus taxes (around $100 total), which is a discount of 10%. To make a reservation call (703) 524-9800 and let them know you are with the Gibbon/Eschbacher wedding party to receive the discounted rate. For more information about the hotel please visit http://innsofva.com/arlington.aspx.

Stuff to do in the area

Some people actually visit the Washington area for reasons other than our wedding. Here is a list of some of our favorite area pastimes/attractions.

  • Hiking
    If you bring a vehicle, we have lots of hikes to recommend in the Blue Ridge Mountains. Here are a couple of our favorite parks—feel free to contact us about specific hikes.
    • Andy Guest State Park — along the Shenandoah River with a range of hikes from easy to moderately difficult (picture)
    • George Washington National Forest — Signal Knob
    • Overall Run & Heiskell Hollow — In Shenandoah National Park but through the backdoor so there is no entry fee. A beautiful hike with wonderful views and waterfalls. Can be difficult but is rewarding. ~12 miles in length.
      Hiking maps and details
    • Shenandoah Nat'l Park
    • For hiking closer to the city check out the Billy Goat Trail

  • National Gallery of Art
    Free government-run art gallery with excellent film series
    NGA’s website
  • Roosevelt Island
    Island in the middle of the Potomac. Nice walk with great views of the DC waterfront.
    Roosevelt Island website
  • National Arboretum
    Our first kiss was under a tree here. Take your honey here and smooch it up.
    Arboretum website
  • Movies
    West End Cinema, E Street Cinema, and AMC Courthouse (right next to the Hilton!)

There is parking on site for up to 70 vehicles. These vehicles can also be left in the parking lot overnight. We will provide shuttles after the wedding beginning at 9:30 pm to the Inns of Virginia and a local Metro stop. More information about the shuttles to come as we book these. If you are staying at the Hilton, shuttle service will be provided to and from the event. Check back later for exact times. Public transportation to and from the event site is also possible. More info here.

What’s the dress code?

There isn’t one. The bride will probably wear a dress and the groom is thinking about buying a second suit. We want you to feel comfortable above all else. The weather these days is anyone’s guess, so we advise planning a versatile outfit. The venue is a public park so shirts and shoes would probably be encouraged by the county, but we couldn’t find anything about that here: http://parks.arlingtonva.us/arlington-county-park-rules-regulations/

Do you have a wedding registry?

We do not. We really truly feel that your presence on our marriage journey, be it physical or spiritual, is the best gift you could give.
